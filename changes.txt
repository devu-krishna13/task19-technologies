Design Comparison: Video vs. webandcrafts.com
1. Summary
The recorded site follows a similar overall structure to webandcrafts.com (WAC) — hero, offerings, proof, team, insights, footer — but WAC executes each section with more visual weight, more consistency, and proof placed earlier on the page. The recorded site is noticeably thinner: fewer sections, a single static hero instead of a rotating one, no client logos or case studies, plain-text stats, and a lighter footer.

3.2 Hero Section [0:10–0:14] — High

Video: One static hero — headline, paragraph, single "Get Free Audit" button, stats packed close beneath.
WAC: Rotating multi-slide hero cycling brand statement → case study metric → event → offer.
Gap: One claim per visit vs. WAC's several angles in the same space.

3.3 Proof / Case Studies Placement [0:14–0:20] — High

Video: Section right after the hero is service-offering cards (a pitch), not finished work.
WAC: Section right after the hero is a 6-card grid of real, named case studies with industry tags.
Gap: Recorded site leads with a pitch before any proof; WAC leads with proof first.

3.4 Stats / Numbers [0:20] — Medium

Video: Four stats in plain text, cramped directly under the sticky header.
WAC: Large-numeral cards with generous padding, placed after the proof section.
Gap: Feels cramped and premature vs. WAC's spacious, earned placement.

3.5 Testimonials [0:20–0:25] — Medium

Video: 3-card carousel, but cards look unevenly filled (two show a closing quote line, one appears cut off).
WAC: 3 evenly-sized cards, each with photo, full quote, name, title, company, industry tag.
Gap: Same idea, inconsistent execution vs. WAC's uniform layout.

3.6 Feature/Value Section [0:40–0:53] — Medium

Video: "Stop Leaving Revenue on the Table" — 3-point pitch next to a dashboard graphic with an artificial, generated look.
WAC: "What We Do" — 6 pillar tiles, each with a custom, purpose-made image.
Gap: Recorded graphic reads as synthetic/stock, undercutting credibility.

3.7 Client Logos — High

Video: No client logos anywhere in the 93-second recording.
WAC: Row of 14 recognizable client logos, prominently placed.
Gap: Clearest gap — a fast, high-value trust element that's entirely absent.

3.8 Blog/Insights Cards [1:20–1:32] — Medium

Video: Abstract, generic thumbnails unrelated to their headlines.
WAC: Imagery tied to the specific article topic.
Gap: Reads as stock filler vs. WAC's curated, editorial feel.

3.9 Repeated CTA Blocks [0:29–0:31, 0:55–0:57, 1:24–1:26] — Medium

Video: Same CTA pair appears to render twice, different sizes/positions, same closing section — seen identically on About, Portfolio, Blog.
WAC: One single, clearly-styled closing CTA per page.
Gap: Two versions of the same action dilutes which button to click; reads as an unresolved layout bug.

3.10 Team Section [1:05] — Low

Video: 4-person grid; third card visibly taller due to extra bio text, breaking row alignment.
WAC: Consistently sized frames regardless of caption length.
Gap: Noticeable alignment inconsistency next to WAC's evenness.

3.11 Footer [1:32–1:33] — High

Video: Logo, short blurb, contact details, 3 link columns, social icons — shorter, less structured.
WAC: Full 4-column footer (Company/Services/Other) + Connect block with multiple contact lines, 7 social icons, Privacy/Terms/Sitemap.
Gap: Recorded footer covers noticeably less ground, no visible legal links.

3.12 Pop-up/Exit Form [1:30] — Medium

Video: Pop-up with lead form + "Trusted by Industry Leaders" shown as plain text, not logos.
WAC: No sitewide exit pop-up; relies on persistent header CTA + closing CTA instead.
Gap: Extra interruption whose trust badges aren't real logos, weakening intended credibility.
